Legal position

Current starter summary

Bosnia and Herzegovina allows limited duty-free tobacco in travellers' personal luggage, but excess tobacco is outside the passenger relief. Once the allowance is exceeded, import duties and other taxes apply and customs can treat undeclared excess as an offence.

Conditions

What would need to be true

The relief is limited to up to 200 cigarettes, or 100 cigarillos, or 50 cigars, or 250 grams of smoking tobacco, or a proportional combination.

Exceptions

Known carve-outs or edge cases

Travellers under 17 do not get the tobacco relief.

Penalties

Penalty snapshot

Excess tobacco can trigger customs duties, VAT, excise and seizure or customs-offence measures if it is not properly declared.

Enforcement

How this may be enforced

Customs officers check the passenger quantities at the border and published customs notices report confiscations for undeclared or controlled goods.
