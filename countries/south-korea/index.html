Starter activities in South Korea

These rows prove the country hub works as a useful browse page before the full dataset is researched.

drive without licence

South Korea’s Road Traffic Act prohibits driving a motor vehicle without obtaining the required driver’s licence. The official penalty article cited for this offence provides up to one year imprisonment or a fine of up to 3 million won.

VehiclesNo

bringing duty free goods

Bringing duty-free goods into South Korea is allowed within the official traveller allowance. Korea Customs says the general passenger-effects allowance is up to US$600, with separate additional allowances for cigarettes, liquor and perfume.

TravelYes

bring electronics without declaring them

Arriving travellers in South Korea must submit a personal effects declaration form, and goods that must be declared have to be declared voluntarily. If declarable goods such as over-allowance electronics are not declared, customs can impose additional duty.

bring food into a country

Food imports are not a flat yes or no traveller rule in South Korea. Korea Customs gives a duty-free allowance for agricultural, forest, fishery and livestock products only if the total weight is under 40 kg, the overseas purchase price is under 100000 won and quarantine inspection is passed.

TravelDepends

bring medical cannabis into a country

South Korea does not allow travellers to bring medicines containing controlled substances into the country without prior approval. The Ministry of Food and Drug Safety says any person entering Korea with such medicines for self-treatment must get a permit from the Ministry.

TravelDepends

bring tobacco over the allowance

South Korea gives a separate traveller tobacco allowance of 200 cigarettes. Tobacco above the allowance is not within the duty-free relief and undeclared declarable goods can attract additional duty.

TravelDepends

buy a pepper spray

South Korea does not treat gas sprayers and electric shock devices as ordinary unrestricted consumer items. The weapons safety law requires a possession permit for gas sprayers and electric shock machines unless a statutory exception applies.
