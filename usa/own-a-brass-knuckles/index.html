Legal position

Current starter summary

The United States does not have one nationwide brass-knuckles rule. Official state sources show state-dependent regulation: California criminalizes manufacturing, importing, keeping for sale, offering for sale, giving, lending, or possessing metal knuckles, while Massachusetts punishes carrying metallic knuckles on the person or under control in a vehicle without legal authorization.

Conditions

What would need to be true

State law governs. California treats possession of metal knuckles as prohibited conduct, while Massachusetts separately punishes carrying or vehicle control; other states may differ materially.

Penalties

Penalty snapshot

Penalties depend on the state law that applies; California and Massachusetts both create state offences in the cited provisions.

Australia

Australia does not have one uniform civilian brass-knuckles rule. Victoria Police classifies a knuckle-duster as a prohibited weapon, and Victoria Police approval material says prohibited weapons generally require Chief Commissioner approval or an applicable exemption for purchase, possession, carriage or transport. Australian Border Force treats knuckle-dusters as weapons subject to border controls.
