Legal position

Current starter summary

In Ireland it is an offence to possess in a public place, without lawful authority or reasonable excuse, an article made or adapted for causing injury or incapacitating a person. Carrying knuckledusters in public therefore exposes the carrier to an offensive-weapons offence.

Conditions

What would need to be true

A lawful authority or reasonable excuse would be needed, and the reviewed official sources do not support ordinary self-defence carriage.

Exceptions

Known carve-outs or edge cases

The offence turns on possession in a public place without lawful authority or reasonable excuse.

Penalties

Penalty snapshot

For the public-place offence, Irish law provides for up to €5,000 or up to 12 months on summary conviction, or a fine and up to 5 years on indictment.

Enforcement

How this may be enforced

Irish offensive-weapons law applies to public-place possession of articles made or adapted to cause injury or incapacitate a person.
