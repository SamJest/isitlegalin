Legal position

Current starter summary

You can bring some food into New Zealand, but it must be declared and many foods are restricted or prohibited for biosecurity reasons.

Conditions

What would need to be true

Declare food on the New Zealand Traveller Declaration and expect inspection on arrival.

Exceptions

Known carve-outs or edge cases

Some foods can be cleared if they meet the official entry conditions, but clearance is not guaranteed.

Penalties

Penalty snapshot

Failing to declare biosecurity risk goods can lead to an instant NZD 400 fine, and deliberate smuggling can lead to prosecution with much heavier penalties.

Enforcement

How this may be enforced

MPI biosecurity officers inspect declared goods at the border and decide whether they can enter New Zealand.
