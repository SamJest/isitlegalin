Legal position

Current starter summary

Keeping brass knuckles at home is unlawful in the United Kingdom because knuckledusters are covered by the private possession ban for offensive weapons.

Conditions

What would need to be true

The row applies to ordinary private possession of modern knuckledusters.

Exceptions

Known carve-outs or edge cases

Antiques over 100 years old and narrow museum or gallery cases are treated differently under the legislation.

Penalties

Penalty snapshot

The Offensive Weapons Act 2019 provides that private possession is an offence and can lead to imprisonment or a fine on summary conviction.

Enforcement

How this may be enforced

Police can seize prohibited offensive weapons held in private and prosecute possession offences.

Australia

Australia does not have one uniform civilian brass-knuckles rule. Victoria Police classifies a knuckle-duster as a prohibited weapon, and Victoria Police approval material says prohibited weapons generally require Chief Commissioner approval or an applicable exemption for purchase, possession, carriage or transport. Australian Border Force treats knuckle-dusters as weapons subject to border controls.
