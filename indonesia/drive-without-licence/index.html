Legal position

Current starter summary

Indonesia requires drivers to hold a valid SIM to drive on the road. Official police guidance says driving without a SIM is a criminal traffic offence.

Conditions

What would need to be true

Obtain and carry the correct SIM before driving.

Exceptions

Known carve-outs or edge cases

No general exception was identified in the official sources reviewed.

Penalties

Penalty snapshot

Official police guidance cites up to 4 months’ imprisonment or a fine of up to Rp1,000,000 for driving without a SIM.
