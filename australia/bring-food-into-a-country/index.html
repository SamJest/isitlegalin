Legal position

Current starter summary

Food can be brought into Australia only if it is declared and the item is allowed under the biosecurity rules because some foods need inspection or cannot enter at all.

Conditions

What would need to be true

Travellers should check the item rules before travel and declare all food on arrival.

Exceptions

Known carve-outs or edge cases

Some foods are allowed after inspection while others are prohibited or need prior permission.

Penalties

Penalty snapshot

ABF says penalties may apply if goods are not declared.

Enforcement

How this may be enforced

Border officials inspect declared food and biosecurity rules determine whether the item can enter.
