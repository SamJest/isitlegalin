Legal position

Current starter summary

Australia does not have one uniform civilian stun-gun rule. Victoria Police classifies a Taser or stun gun as a prohibited weapon, and Victoria Police approval material says prohibited weapons generally require Chief Commissioner approval or an applicable exemption for purchase, possession, carriage or transport. Australian Border Force customs material treats handheld electric-shock devices, including Tasers, stun guns and stun batons, as arms.

Conditions

What would need to be true

State or territory law governs domestic purchase, possession, carriage and transport. In Victoria, prohibited-weapon activities generally require Chief Commissioner approval unless an exemption applies.

Exceptions

Known carve-outs or edge cases

Any lawful pathway depends on the relevant state or territory approval or exemption.

Penalties

Penalty snapshot

Specific penalties depend on the applicable state or territory offence and whether any approval or exemption exists.

Enforcement

How this may be enforced

State and territory police enforce domestic weapons laws; the Australian Border Force enforces import controls.
