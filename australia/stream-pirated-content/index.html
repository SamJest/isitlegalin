Legal position

Current starter summary

The official Australian copyright material checked here does not support a simple consumer-facing yes or no answer for streaming pirated content even though it clearly says copyright owners control acts such as making content available online and other communications of copyright material.

Conditions

What would need to be true

Authorized licensed streaming services are outside this row and the answer changes if the source is rights-holder authorized.

Exceptions

Known carve-outs or edge cases

The reviewed official material does not resolve every ordinary viewer streaming scenario into a flat national rule from these sources alone.

Penalties

Penalty snapshot

The official sources used here do not set one fixed penalty for a generic consumer streaming row.

Enforcement

How this may be enforced

IP Australia says copyright owners have exclusive rights over acts including communicating copyright material such as broadcasting it or making it available online and the Copyright Act says infringement occurs where a person does an act comprised in the copyright without licence.
