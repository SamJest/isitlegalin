What happens if I carry pepper spray in the United Kingdom?

Structured guidance for United Kingdom | Updated 1 January 2026

Illegal High risk Source trail 2

Decision summary

No. Carrying pepper spray is generally illegal in the United Kingdom, unless a narrow exception or legal justification clearly applies.

Start from prohibition and only move off that position if a clear exception fits your facts.

Pepper spray is treated as a prohibited weapon in the UK and cannot be carried or owned by civilians for self-defence. Use the penalties and enforcement notes below to judge the practical downside of getting this wrong.

FAQ

Common questions

What happens if I carry pepper spray in the United Kingdom?

No. Carrying pepper spray is generally illegal in the United Kingdom, unless a narrow exception or legal justification clearly applies.

What conditions matter for carrying pepper spray in the United Kingdom?

Classed as a prohibited weapon under UK law. Self-defence is not a valid reason for possession. Importing or purchasing is also restricted.

Are there exceptions for carrying pepper spray in the United Kingdom?

Authorised police use only. Inert or non-functional training sprays may differ.

What are the penalties for carrying pepper spray in the United Kingdom?

Unlimited fine possible. Up to several years imprisonment. Considered a serious weapons offence.

How is carrying pepper spray enforced in the United Kingdom?

Police and border officials actively seize and prosecute possession cases.
