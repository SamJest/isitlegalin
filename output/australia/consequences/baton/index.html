What happens if I carry a baton in Australia?

Structured guidance for Australia | Updated 1 January 2026

Illegal High risk Source trail 2

Decision summary

No. Carrying a baton is generally illegal in Australia, unless a narrow exception or legal justification clearly applies.

Start from prohibition and only move off that position if a clear exception fits your facts.

Batons, particularly expandable types, are generally prohibited weapons in Australia and cannot be carried by civilians. Use the penalties and enforcement notes below to judge the practical downside of getting this wrong.

Common questions

What happens if I carry a baton in Australia?

No. Carrying a baton is generally illegal in Australia, unless a narrow exception or legal justification clearly applies.

What conditions matter for carrying a baton in Australia?

Classified as prohibited in most states. No lawful self-defence use for civilians. Possession without permit is illegal.

Are there exceptions for carrying a baton in Australia?

Police or authorised security use. Limited permits in rare cases.

What are the penalties for carrying a baton in Australia?

Significant fines. Possible imprisonment. Strict controls apply.

How is carrying a baton enforced in Australia?

Police seize batons and enforce state-level weapon restrictions.
