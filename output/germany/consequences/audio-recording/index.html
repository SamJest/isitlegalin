What happens if I record conversations in Germany?

Structured guidance for Germany | Updated 1 January 2026

Illegal High risk Source trail 2

Decision summary

No. Recording conversations is generally illegal in Germany, unless a narrow exception or legal justification clearly applies.

Start from prohibition and only move off that position if a clear exception fits your facts.

Recording private conversations without consent is illegal in Germany, even if you are part of the conversation. Use the penalties and enforcement notes below to judge the practical downside of getting this wrong.

FAQ

Common questions

What happens if I record conversations in Germany?

No. Recording conversations is generally illegal in Germany, unless a narrow exception or legal justification clearly applies.

What conditions matter for recording conversations in Germany?

All parties must consent to recording. Private conversations are strongly protected. Secret recordings are prohibited.

Are there exceptions for recording conversations in Germany?

Public speeches or events may be recorded. Law enforcement may record under legal authority.

What are the penalties for recording conversations in Germany?

Fines under criminal law. Possible imprisonment. Germany has strict two-party consent rules.

How is recording conversations enforced in Germany?

Violations are often prosecuted when recordings are discovered or reported.
