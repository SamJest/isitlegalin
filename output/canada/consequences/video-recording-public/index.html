What happens if I record people in public in Canada?

Structured guidance for Canada | Updated 1 January 2026

Decision summary

Yes. Recording people in public is generally legal in Canada, although specific conditions or restrictions may still apply.

The baseline answer is permissive, but the listed conditions and practical limits still matter.

Recording video in public places is generally allowed in Canada, as long as it does not intrude on reasonable expectations of privacy or involve harassment. Use the penalties and enforcement notes below to judge the practical downside of getting this wrong.
